About Me

I'm a designer, developer, and researcher slated to graduate from The University of Chicago in June 2017. I combine my loves of art and technology to make a better world. I harness my dual skill sets of development and design to create user experiences, and digital products. Above all, my design philosophy revolves around empathy - for the user, for the people I work with, and for society. I practice these ends through engaging projects in data visualization, product design, user experience, digital humanities, and new media. I am particularly interested in applications to museology, civic tech, and education.

My research informs my practice, and it focuses on interactive media, critical design, and femininity on the internet. I'm an expert on designing games for social activism, and digital feminine knowledge networks - or, how women use the internet to share content. I'm also an advocate for marginalized populations, and strive to lift up women, people of color, disabled persons and queer perspectives in all my activities.

At The University of Chicago, I run three student organizations (two focusing on women in tech!) in addition to working part time to support myself. I've authored by own degree program in Design, Media, and Technology, featuring coursework in anthropology, art, computer science, and media studies.

I'm always on the lookout for opportunities that blend art, technology, and social good. I'm planning on graduating in June 2017, and am actively seeking internship and full-time opportunities.

I'd love to chat about any projects or ideas, and I enjoy meeting new people. My email is jazzmithani [at] gmail.com, and you can follow me on Twitter @jazzmyth. My full work history is on LinkedIn, and you can checkout what I'm working on at my Github page.
