Feed the Baby

research / development / testing


Hi! This page is still a work in progress. I have included some notes about the project, and links where appropriate. If you want to know why I have been so busy lately, check out my Twitter and my LinkedIn!

Feed the Baby was a very quick project (approximately one month development time) put together to show an alternate use of digital games at DoSomething.org. My manager and I had been spending most of our time on Words Matter, our idea for a standalone product, and wanted to prove that games could fit into other spaces in our organization. Our first thought was to revamp the "11 Facts About Teenage Pregnancy" webpage, which remained mostly static throughout the year with a link to a low-conversion campaign. We originally wanted to try something along the lines of an Explorable Explanation, so that DoSomething members could understand the impact that teenage pregnancy had on education, and poverty. By reframing these facts, we could encourage members to participate in a campaign to make a difference offline.

As the end of 2015 approached, we decided to focus on development tools we already knew (Phaser, as opposed to HTML Canvas) to create a game that added interactivity and pushed to a campaign page. We also added a way for players to share their scores via Twitter. So far we have completed three rounds of A/B testing with Optimize.ly, and have most recently seen a 542% increase in users clicking through to a campaign.
