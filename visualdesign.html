Visual Design

> background

My mother is a painter, and under her tutelage I've studied art technique from a young age. I switched between after school lessons, and private sessions with her out on our home patio. As I got older, the formal training was put on hold until I signed up for a college-level 2D Design class to complement my interest in user interfaces. That class changed the trajectory of my life by introducing me to nomenclature and theory that would guide me beyond the paper cuts and painting we produced in the classroom.

Today, my sketchbook is my Swiss Army Knife: always with me, indispensible, and full of unexpected things. Whenever I read about a new design principle I pull out my pencil and sketch an application; if I think of an interesting argument for an article I write it down; and I am constantly working on designs for products I want to (or have!) made.

Below are some examples of works done in a classroom environment and on my own. Oftentimes, I write notes on interaction or ideas alongside my sketches, which provide insight into my thought process.
